What is Compound Interest?

Learning Objectives

  • Explain terminologies (periods, interest, principal amount, interest rate, time, and amount) related to compound interest.
  • Calculate the final value of compound interest with a dependent variable by using the given independent variables.

Case study

Donovan plans to invest his money into a bank to earn some interest. The bank staff tells him if he wants to deposit for a long time he can choose a compound interest plan. He deposits $1,000 at 12% per year compounded per month. How much interest will be paid from our account after 16 months?

Solutions

To calculate the interest based on compound interest, you should first figure out the concept of“period”, which is a time variable referring to how many time units are in this investment. Then, you can calculate interest rate each period (i), and how many periods for your savings (n).

  1. Periods number: n

    →

    n = 16 as each month is one period

  2. Interest rate each period: i = r / m

    →

    i = r / m = 12% / = 1%

  3. Interest rate per year: r

    →

    r = 12%

  4. Compounded times per year: m

    →

    m = 12

  5. Calculate the A= Amount (future value) with the formula: A = P+ I

    →

    A = 300 + 45 = 345

Step 2: m = 12

After you figure out the period variables, you can calculate the amount by the formula A(n) = P(1 + i)^n. And the overall interest is the value I = A(n) - P.

  1. Principal (initial): P

    →

    P = 1000

  2. Amount: A(n) = P(1 +i)^n

    →

    A(n) = P(1 +i)^n = (1+1%)^16 = 1172.6

  3. Interests: I = A(n) - P

    →

    I = A(n) - P = 1172.6 - 1000 = 172.6

Step 2: P = 1000

Summary

In this worked example, we learn more variables:

  • Periods number: n
  • Interest rate each period: i = r/m
  • Interest rate per year: r
  • Compounded times per year: m
  • The amount in the account after n periods: A(n)

And the formula we have used:

  • If an initial principal P is invested at an interest rate r compounded m times per year, then the amount in the account after n periods is A(n) = P(1 +i)^n.
  • Interest: I = A(n) - P

Practices with Simple and Compound Interests

Question 1

Imagine you loan $2,000 at 6% per year compounded per month.

a. How many periods are there in ten months?

b. How many interest rates each period?

c. How much will be paid from your account after ten months?

n = 10, as each month is one period.
Interest rate per year “r” is 6%, compounded times per year “m” is 12.
Initial principal is 2000. Combined with the variables above, we can calculate the principal amount A(n) = P(1+i)^n = 2000 (1+0.5%)^10 = 2102.3.

Question 2

Suppose you deposit $3,000 at 12% per year compounded per month. How much will be paid from your account after 8 months?

Interest rate per year “r” is 12%, compounded times per year “m” is 12; As a result, the interest rates i = r / m = 12% / 12 = 1%. Combined with the Initial principal “p” is 3000, we can calculate the principal amount A(n) = P(1 + i )^ n = 3000 (1+1%)^ 8 = 3248.5.

Question 3

You invest $500 at 6% simple interest for one year. Ivy invests $500 at a rate of 6% compounded monthly for one year.

How much money will Ivy have after one year?

Interest rate per year “r” is 6%; investment time “t” is 1; Initial principal “p” is 500. As a result, we can calculate the principal amount A= 500*(1+6%*1) = 530.
Interest rate per year “r” is 6%, compounded times per year “m” is 12; As a result, the interest rates i = r / m = 6% / 12 = 0.5%. Combined with the Initial principal “p” is 500, we can calculate the principal amount A(n) = P(1 + i )^ n = 500 (1+1%)^ 12 = 563.4.
