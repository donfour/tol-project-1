What is Simple Interest?

Learning Objectives

  • Explain terminologies (interest, principal amount, interest rate, time, and amount) related to simple interest.
  • Calculate the final value of simple interest with a dependent variable by using the given independent variables.

Case study

Rachel earned $300 from performing piano in a community concert. She then put the money into a bank savings account. The bank manager told her the interest rate is 15% at the beginning of the year. At the end of the year, Rachel went to the bank to check her account, how much money will be in her bank account?

Practices

Question 1

You invest x money in the bank at a simple interest rate of y. How much interest will you earn after z years?

You earned interest after 2 years.

The principal amount “p” is x, the Interest rate “r” is y, the time for saving “t” is z.

As a result, the interest is: I = x * y * z

Question 2

You invest $ 6500 in Chase at a simple interest rate of 8%.

a. How much will be in your bank account after 3 years?

There will be in my bank account after 3 years.

b. How about 5 years?

There will be in my bank account after 5 years.

a. The principal amount “p” is 6500, the Interest rate “r” is 8%, the saving duration “t” is 3. We can apply the formula to calculate the future value: A = 6500 * ( 1 + 8% * 3) = 8060.

b. The same to the former question, this question requires you to save 5 years. Therefore the final money in the bank account is: A = 6500 * ( 1 + 8 % * 5 ) = 9100.

Question 3

You are planning to see a Broadway musical with your roommate Rachel after 2 years you all graduate from CMU. The ticket price is $150 for each person. You and Rachel opened a shared Account in the local bank with an 10% interest rate. Therefore, how much money should you and Rachel put into the account now?

Rachel and I should put into our account now.

Rachel and I need 150*2 for watching the musical. so $300 will be the future value “A”.

They can spend 2 years to save money as time “t” while the interest rate “r” is 10%. Therefore, we can apply reverse formula to calculate the principal amount “p”: p = A / ( 1 + r * t ).

The principal amount is: 150 * 2 / ( 1 + 10% * 2 ) = 250.

Summary

In this worked example, we will find several variables:

  • I=interest
  • p= principal amount
  • r=interest rate
  • t=time (years)
  • A= Amount (future value)

And the formula we have used:

  • If a principal amount P is invested at an interest rate r for t years, then the simple interest earned will be I = Prt;
  • The future value amount will be A = P+ I;
  • One-step formula to calculate future value: A= P(1 + rt)
